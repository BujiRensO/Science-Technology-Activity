My Weekly Carbon Footprint Breakdown

Transportation

Overview of Weekly Commute

Mode of Transport: Motorcycle

Purpose: Daily commute to school

Distance Traveled:

  • Per Trip: 1 kilometer (one-way)
  • Daily Total: 2 kilometers
  • Weekly Total: 10 kilometers (5 weekdays)

Carbon Emission Calculation

Emission Factor for Motorcycles: 0.103 kg CO₂ per kilometer (average)

Weekly Carbon Footprint:

10 km × 0.103 kg CO₂/km = 1.03 kg CO₂

Impact Analysis

Key Insights:

  • Minimal emissions due to the short commute distance.
  • Still contributes to overall carbon footprint, which can be reduced with alternative methods of travel.

Comparison:

  • Emissions are less than driving a car for the same distance (0.171 kg CO₂/km).
  • Walking or cycling would result in zero emissions.

Energy Use Assessment

My Daily Device Usage Report

Smartphone

  • Charging Frequency: 2 times per day
  • Average Charging Time: 1.5 hours per charge
  • Power Consumption: 5 watts (average smartphone charging power)
  • Total Daily Usage: 2 charges/day × 1.5 hours/charge × 5 watts = 15 watt-hours/day

Laptop

  • Daily Usage: 5 hours
  • Power Consumption: 60 watts (average laptop power draw)
  • Total Daily Usage: 5 hours/day × 60 watts = 300 watt-hours/day

Weekly Energy Use

  • Smartphone: 15 watt-hours/day × 7 days = 105 watt-hours/week
  • Laptop: 300 watt-hours/day × 7 days = 2100 watt-hours/week
  • Total Weekly Energy Use: 105 watt-hours + 2100 watt-hours = 2205 watt-hours (or 2.205 kWh)

Carbon Footprint of Energy Use

  • Emission Factor: 0.475 kg CO₂ per kWh (average grid electricity worldwide)
  • Weekly Emissions: 2.205 kWh × 0.475 kg CO₂/kWh = 1.05 kg CO₂

Impact Analysis

  • Devices Contributing to Carbon Footprint:
    • Laptop is the largest contributor due to higher power consumption.
    • Smartphone has a smaller impact but still adds up over time.
  • Potential Savings:
    • Switching to a laptop with better energy efficiency could reduce emissions.
    • Using solar chargers for your phone could eliminate related carbon emissions.

Diet Carbon Footprint Assessment

My Week Food Consumption Report

Food: Rice

  • Meal Frequency: 3 meals per day
  • Accompanied By: Traditional Filipino dishes such as vegetables, fish, pork, or chicken
  • Rice Consumption: 1 cup (158 grams) per meal

Rice Consumption

  • Carbon Emission Factor: 0.4 kg CO₂ per kg of cooked rice
  • Daily Emissions: 3 meals/day × 158g × 0.4 kg CO₂/kg = 0.19 kg CO₂/day
  • Weekly Emissions: 0.19 kg CO₂/day × 7 days = 1.33 kg CO₂/week

Filipino Dishes: Fish

  • Average Serving: 100g per meal
  • Emission Factor: 1.34 kg CO₂ per kg (for tilapia)
  • Weekly Emissions: 3 meals/day × 0.1 kg × 1.34 kg CO₂/kg × 7 days = 2.81 kg CO₂

Filipino Dishes: Pork/Chicken

  • Pork Emission Factor: 5.83 kg CO₂ per kg
  • Chicken Emission Factor: 1.65 kg CO₂ per kg
  • Weekly Emissions (Pork): 0.1 × 5.83 × 3 = 1.75 kg CO₂
  • Weekly Emissions (Chicken): 0.1 × 1.65 × 4 = 0.66 kg CO₂

Vegetables

  • Average Serving: 100g per meal
  • Emission Factor: 0.1 kg CO₂ per kg
  • Weekly Emissions: 3 meals/day × 0.1 kg × 0.1 kg CO₂/kg × 7 days = 0.21 kg CO₂

Total Weekly Carbon Footprint

  • Rice: 1.33 kg CO₂
  • Fish: 2.81 kg CO₂
  • Pork/Chicken: 2.41 kg CO₂
  • Vegetables: 0.21 kg CO₂
  • Total: 6.76 kg CO₂

Impact Analysis

  • Key Contributors:
    • Meat dishes contribute significantly due to high emissions associated with meat production.
    • Rice, while a staple, has a relatively low footprint compared to meat.
  • Comparison:
    • Diets with more vegetables and less meat tend to have lower carbon footprints.

Overall Conclusion and Reflection

Conclusion

Commuting

With a carbon footprint of 1.03 kg CO₂ per week from motorcycle use, your short commute contributes a small amount to your overall emissions. However, even minor reductions, such as using a more energy-efficient vehicle, walking, or cycling, could eliminate these emissions entirely, making your daily travel even more sustainable.

Energy Use

The 2.205 kWh of electricity consumed by your phone and laptop translates into 1.05 kg CO₂ in emissions. This is a moderate impact, with potential for improvement. By optimizing charging habits, using energy-efficient devices, or powering your devices with renewable energy, you could significantly lower the carbon footprint of your energy use.

Diet

The largest contribution to your weekly carbon footprint comes from your diet, particularly from meat-based dishes, which account for 6.76 kg CO₂. Shifting towards more plant-based meals and reducing the consumption of high-emission foods like pork could dramatically lower your environmental impact. The benefits of a plant-based diet are not just limited to reducing emissions—they also promote better health and reduce strain on other environmental resources like water and land.

Reflection

For this particular exercise, I have calculated my weekly carbon footprint which is not very high indeed, but it has also made me realize how minor changes in our daily routines can make a much bigger contribution to the overall pollution in the long run. When looked at together, cutting back on emissions in travel, energy consumption, and diet add up to a favorable ripple effect not only for my footprint reduction but for healthier living.

I feel personally responsible for every single choice I make in my everyday life, be it selecting a green mode of transport, turning lights off every time I leave a room, or choosing healthier foods that make a positive impact on the global climate. The principal message in this case is that even the tiniest action is significant within the broader spectrum of progress towards sustainability.

Through observing small changes in my behavior and adjusting my action gradually, I am doing my best to reduce my contribution to the emission of greenhouse gases. They may all appear quite minor, week by week, but, collectively, they add up to a great improvement to the environment. As you’ve seen, every action helps, and millions of people like me are the driving force behind change for the better.
